📘 Module 11: Business Analytics & Decision Making

Learn how to use your business data to make smart, confident decisions

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand what business analytics means for small businesses
  • Collect useful data from sales, customers, and costs
  • Turn numbers into insights (e.g., what’s selling, what’s not)
  • Use data to improve pricing, inventory, and marketing
  • Make decisions based on facts — not guesses

📊 1. What Is Business Analytics?

Business analytics means using real information from your business to make better choices.

You don’t need complex software — just a few simple records.

  • How many products did you sell this week?
  • Which item made the most profit?
  • What do customers say about your service?

Example: You sell 5 products. Analytics tells you that muffins make 60% of your profit — so you should bake more muffins!

📈 2. Track the Right Data (Simple & Practical)

You don’t need to track everything — just what matters.

Key Data to Collect Weekly:
  • Sales by Product: How many of each item did you sell?
  • Revenue: Total money earned
  • Costs: Stock, transport, packaging
  • Profit: Revenue – Costs
  • Customer Feedback: What did they like or complain about?
💡 Start small: Just write down 3 things every Friday: “Top seller, biggest cost, one customer comment.”

🔍 3. Ask the Right Questions

Data is only useful if you ask good questions.

  • What’s selling well? → Make more of it.
  • What’s not selling? → Stop wasting money on it.
  • When are you busiest? → Plan staffing or promotions.
  • Which customers return? → Find more like them.
  • Are costs rising? → Can you find cheaper supplies?

Golden Rule: Don’t guess. Check the numbers.

📉 4. Use Simple Reports to See Trends

You don’t need graphs — just a simple table.

Weekly Sales Tracker:

Week Muffins Sold Vetkoek Sold Total Profit
Week 1 30 50 R850
Week 2 45 40 R920
Week 3 60 35 R1,100

Insight: Muffin sales are going up. Maybe run a “Buy 2, Get 1 Free” promo to grow it faster!

✅ 5. Make Better Decisions with Data

Let’s see how data leads to action:

  • Problem: “I’m busy but not making much money.”
    Data: Vetkoek sells a lot but has low profit (R2 each). Muffins sell less but earn R8 profit each.
    Decision: Focus on selling more muffins, maybe raise vetkoek price slightly.
  • Problem: “I don’t know who my customers are.”
    Data: 80% of muffin buyers are office workers between 7–9 AM.
    Decision: Arrive earlier and bring more muffins on weekday mornings.

🔄 6. Review & Improve Weekly

Set a weekly routine:

  1. Every Friday, review your sales and expenses.
  2. Ask: “What worked? What didn’t?”
  3. Make one small change for next week.
  4. Repeat.
“Success isn’t about being perfect. It’s about learning one thing each week and getting a little better.”
