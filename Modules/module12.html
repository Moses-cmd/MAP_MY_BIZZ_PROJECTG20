📘 Module 12: Personal Finance & Money Management

Learn how to manage your personal money wisely — even when business income changes

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between business income and personal spending
  • Create a simple household budget
  • Pay yourself fairly from your business
  • Build an emergency fund for personal and business needs
  • Avoid overspending during good months

💼 1. Business Money vs. Personal Money

Just because money comes into your business doesn’t mean it’s yours to spend.

  • Business Income: Money earned from sales — must cover costs, taxes, and reinvestment.
  • Personal Income: Money you take home to pay for food, rent, school, transport.

Golden Rule: Always separate your business and personal money — even if it’s just two envelopes or two bank accounts.

💡 Tip: Decide in advance how much you will “pay yourself” each week or month — like a salary.

📝 2. Create a Simple Household Budget

A budget helps you live within your means and avoid stress.

Track your **monthly personal expenses**:

Expense Amount (R)
Rent / Home R1,500
Electricity & Water R400
Food & Groceries R1,200
School / Children R300
Transport R600
Phone & Internet R300
Miscellaneous R200
Total Monthly Expenses R4,500

You need to earn at least R4,500 per month for personal needs — plan your business to reach this goal.

💰 3. Pay Yourself a Regular “Salary”

Don’t take money randomly. Set a fixed amount you withdraw from the business each week or month.

  • If your business is new: Start small (e.g., R500/week)
  • As profits grow: Increase your pay gradually
  • Only pay yourself after covering business costs
Example: Sipho takes R1,200 every Friday from his repair shop profits. He uses this for home expenses. The rest stays in the business for tools and stock.

🛡️ 4. Build an Emergency Fund

Life happens — illness, car breakdown, slow sales. Be ready.

  • Goal: Save 1–3 months of personal expenses (e.g., R4,500 → R13,500)
  • Start Small: Save R20, R50, or R100 per week
  • Keep It Safe: In a separate bank account or stokvel

Rule: Only use this money for real emergencies — not for shopping or parties.

💡 Challenge: This month, save just R100 in your “Emergency Jar.” Next month, try R200.

📉 5. Avoid Overspending When Business is Good

It’s tempting to spend more when you have a great week.

But irregular income means good months must cover bad ones.

  • Don’t upgrade your lifestyle too fast (new phone, big party)
  • Ask: “Can I afford this every month?” If not, wait.
  • Use extra profits to grow your business or save for the future

Example: You made R3,000 in one week. That’s great! But if your average is R1,500, don’t start spending like you earn R3,000 every week.

📈 6. Plan for Growth & Stability

Good personal finance = freedom to grow your business.

  • Step 1: Cover your basic home needs
  • Step 2: Save a small emergency fund
  • Step 3: Reinvest in your business
  • Step 4: Save for bigger goals (car, home, education)

Remember: A stable home life helps you run a better business.
