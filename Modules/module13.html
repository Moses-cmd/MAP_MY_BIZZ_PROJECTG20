📘 Module 13: Business Communication & Negotiation

Learn how to speak confidently, clearly, and professionally in every business situation

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Communicate clearly with customers, suppliers, and partners
  • Write professional messages via WhatsApp, SMS, or email
  • Negotiate better prices, payment terms, or deals
  • Handle difficult conversations with confidence
  • Build trust through honest, respectful communication

💬 1. Clear Communication Builds Trust

How you speak and write affects how seriously people take your business.

  • Be Clear: Say what you mean. Avoid confusion.
  • Be Polite: “Please” and “Thank you” go a long way.
  • Be Prompt: Reply to messages within 24 hours if possible.

Example:
❌ “I’ll bring it when I can.”
✅ “I’ll deliver your order tomorrow between 10 AM and 12 PM.”

📱 2. Professional Messaging (WhatsApp, SMS, Email)

You don’t need to sound formal — just clear and respectful.

Tips for Writing Messages:
  • Start with a greeting: “Hi Mr. Jacobs, this is Thandi from Thandi’s Bakery.”
  • Be direct: “I’m following up on the flour order we discussed.”
  • End clearly: “Please let me know if this works for you.”
Hi Nomsa,
This is Jabu from Soweto Repairs. Just confirming your phone is ready for pickup today between 3–5 PM.
Total: R350 cash on collection.
Thanks for trusting us with your repair!

🤝 3. How to Negotiate Like a Pro

Negotiation means discussing terms so both sides win.

You can negotiate:

  • Prices with suppliers (“Can I get a discount if I buy more?”)
  • Payment terms (“Can I pay half now, half later?”)
  • Delivery times (“Can you deliver by Thursday?”)
Negotiation Tips:
  • Do Your Research: Know the fair price before you ask.
  • Be Respectful: “I really like your product. Is there any flexibility on price?”
  • Offer Value: “I’ll pay cash today if you can reduce the price by 10%.”

🛒 Real-Life Example: Negotiating Stock Prices

You: “Hi, I’m starting a small snack business. I’d like to buy 100 packets of chips. The usual price is R8, but I see others get R7 in bulk. Can I get R7 if I pay cash today?”

Supplier: “Hmm… if you take 120 packets, yes.”

You: “Deal. I’ll come now with cash.”

✅ You saved R120. The supplier made a bigger sale. Win-win!

🗣️ 4. Handling Difficult Conversations

Sometimes you need to talk about tough things — late payments, bad quality, or complaints.

Stay Calm & Professional:
  • Don’t blame: Use “I” statements. “I noticed the delivery was late.”
  • Focus on the solution: “How can we fix this?”
  • Listen first: Let the other person explain.
💡 Practice in your head: “I am calm. I am professional. I deserve respect.”

📞 5. Phone & In-Person Communication Tips

  • Speak clearly: Don’t rush. Pause between sentences.
  • Smile while talking: It changes your tone and makes you sound friendlier.
  • Prepare: Know what you want to say before calling.
  • Take notes: Write down agreements (e.g., “Delivery on Friday at 2 PM”)

Pro Tip: Record important agreements in a WhatsApp message after the call: “Just confirming — we agreed on R900, paid 50% upfront.”

🌟 6. Build Confidence Over Time

You don’t need to be perfect — just consistent.

  • Start small: Practice one professional message per week
  • Learn from each conversation
  • Ask for feedback: “Was my quote clear? Is there anything else you need?”

Remember: Every great business owner started where you are — learning as they went.
