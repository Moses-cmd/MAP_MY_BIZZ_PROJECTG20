📘 Module 6: Accounting Fundamentals

Learn the basics of business accounting — no experience needed!

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand what accounting is and why it matters
  • Learn key terms like income, expenses, profit, and assets
  • Know the difference between personal and business money
  • Keep simple, accurate financial records
  • Use basic accounting to make better business decisions

🧾 1. What Is Accounting?

Accounting is the process of recording, organizing, and understanding your business money.

It’s not just for accountants — every business owner needs to know the basics.

  • Why it matters:
    • Know if you’re making a profit
    • Prepare for tax season
    • Apply for loans or grants
    • Spot problems early (e.g., rising costs)

Good news: You don’t need a degree — just consistency and honesty.

💰 2. Key Accounting Terms Made Simple

  • Income (Revenue): Money your business earns from sales.
  • Expenses: Money you spend to run your business (e.g., stock, transport, rent).
  • Profit: Income – Expenses = Profit (or loss if negative).
  • Assets: Things your business owns (e.g., cash, equipment, inventory).
  • Liabilities: What your business owes (e.g., loans, unpaid bills).
  • Capital (Owner’s Equity): Your investment in the business.

Simple Example:
You sell vetkoek for R10 each. You sell 50 in a day → R500 income.
You spent R200 on flour, oil, and packaging → R300 profit for the day.

📌 3. Keep Business & Personal Money Separate

Mixing money is one of the biggest mistakes small business owners make.

  • Open a Business Bank Account: Even if it’s just a separate wallet or envelope at first.
  • Track Every Transaction: Write down every sale and expense — daily!
  • Save Receipts: Use a folder or take photos with your phone.

Tip: Use a simple notebook or free app like Wave or 1Money to track your money.

📒 4. The Simple Record-Keeping System

You don’t need complex software. Start with this basic table:

Date Description Income (R) Expenses (R) Balance (R)
01/04 Started Business 1,000 0 1,000
02/04 Bought Flour & Oil 0 300 700
02/04 Sales 500 0 1,200

Update this daily. It helps you see your progress and prepare for tax time.

📊 5. Use Accounting to Make Smart Decisions

Your records are not just for tax — they help you grow!

  • What’s Selling? Track which products make the most profit.
  • Where Are You Spending Too Much? If expenses are rising, find ways to cut costs.
  • When to Expand? If you’ve made consistent profit for 3+ months, you may be ready.

Ask Yourself Weekly:
• How much did I earn?
• How much did I spend?
• Did I make a profit?
• What can I improve?
