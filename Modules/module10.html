📘 Module 10: Business Planning – From Idea to Action

Turn your business idea into a clear, simple plan you can follow

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Write a clear description of your business idea
  • Identify your ideal customer
  • Set real, achievable goals
  • Plan your first steps
  • Use your business plan to stay focused and grow

📝 1. What Is a Business Plan?

A **business plan** is not a long document for banks — it’s your **roadmap**.

It answers: “What am I doing, who am I doing it for, and how will I start?”

  • Helps you think clearly
  • Keeps you focused
  • Shows others (family, partners) what you’re building

Good news: Your plan can fit on one page!

👥 2. Who Is Your Customer?

You can’t sell to “everyone.” Be specific.

  • Ask:
    • Who needs my product?
    • Where do they live or shop?
    • What problem does my product solve for them?

Example:
Not: “I sell snacks.”
Better: “I sell healthy muffins to office workers in Braamfontein who want quick breakfasts.”

📌 3. Define Your Business in One Sentence

Fill in the blanks:

I help [customer] to [solve problem] by [what you do].

Example: "I help busy mothers in Soweto eat fresh vegetables by delivering weekly veggie boxes."

Write your own version in a notebook — this is your business in a nutshell.

🎯 4. Set SMART Goals

Your goals should be:

  • Specific
  • Measurable
  • Achievable
  • Relevant
  • Time-bound

Bad Goal: “Make more money.”
SMART Goal: “Sell 30 vetkoek per day for R15 each by the end of this month.”

🚀 5. Your First 5 Action Steps

Big dreams start with small steps. List your first 5 actions:

  1. Buy ingredients for first batch
  2. Make samples and test with friends
  3. Create a WhatsApp business message
  4. Post photos on Instagram
  5. Take first 5 orders

Tip: Do one step at a time. Celebrate each one!

📊 6. Track & Review Your Progress

Your plan is alive — update it every month.

  • What’s working?
  • What’s not?
  • What new goal should I set?

Ask yourself weekly:
“Did I take at least one step forward?”

If yes — you’re growing.
