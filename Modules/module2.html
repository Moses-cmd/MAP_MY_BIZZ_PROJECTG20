üíº 1. What Is a Business?

A business is an organization that provides goods or services to customers in exchange for money.

  • Examples: Bakery, mobile phone repair shop, tutoring service, online store
  • Key Elements:
    • Customers: People who buy your product/service
    • Value: Solving a problem or fulfilling a need
    • Revenue: Money earned from sales

üè¢ 2. Types of Business Structures

How you set up your business affects taxes, liability, and growth.

  • Sole Proprietorship: Owned by one person. Easy to start, full control, but unlimited personal liability.
  • Partnership: Two or more people share ownership. Good for pooling skills and resources.
  • Pty (Ltd): A private company. Limited liability, more formal setup, better for growth.

üöÄ 3. The Role of the Entrepreneur

An entrepreneur identifies opportunities and takes risks to build a business.

  • Key Traits: Creativity, resilience, problem-solving, customer focus
  • Responsibilities:
    • Finding customers
    • Managing money
    • Hiring help (if needed)
    • Adapting to change

‚úÖ 4. Why Do Businesses Succeed or Fail?

Understanding success factors helps you avoid common pitfalls.

  • Success Factors:
    • Strong customer demand
    • Good financial management
    • Clear value proposition
    • Passion and persistence
  • Common Reasons for Failure:
    • Not enough customers
    • Poor cash flow
    • Lack of planning
    • Ignoring customer feedback
