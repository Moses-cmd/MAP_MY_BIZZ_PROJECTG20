📘 Module 15: Basics of Marketing

Learn how to attract customers, stand out, and grow your business — no matter how small you are

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand what marketing really means
  • Use the 4 P’s of marketing: Product, Price, Place, Promotion
  • Identify your target customer
  • Create a unique selling point (USP)
  • Use simple, low-cost ways to promote your business

📢 1. What Is Marketing?

Marketing is everything you do to get customers to know, like, and trust your business.

It’s not just ads — it’s:

  • Your product quality
  • Your pricing
  • Your packaging and branding
  • How you talk to customers
  • Where you sell
💡 Marketing starts the moment someone sees your sign, hears your name, or tastes your food.

📦 2. The 4 P’s of Marketing (Simple & Practical)

Use this framework to plan your marketing strategy:

1. Product
What are you selling? How is it different? Why should people buy it?
2. Price
Is it affordable? Does it reflect quality? Can you offer small sizes or bundles?
3. Place
Where do customers buy it? At your stall, WhatsApp, home delivery, local shop?
4. Promotion
How do people find out? Word-of-mouth, WhatsApp, posters, Google Maps?

👥 3. Know Your Customer

You can’t market to “everyone.” Be specific.

  • Ask:
    • Who buys from me now?
    • What do they need or care about?
    • Where do they spend time (online or in person)?

Example:
Not: “I sell juice.”
Better: “I sell healthy fruit juice to gym-goers and busy moms who want natural drinks.”

🌟 4. Create a Unique Selling Point (USP)

Your USP is why customers should choose YOU over others.

  • “Freshly baked every morning”
  • “No sugar added”
  • “Ready in 10 minutes”
  • “Cheapest in the area”
  • “Home delivery included”

Tip: Write your USP in one sentence: “We are the only [type of business] that [what makes you special].”

📣 5. Low-Cost Ways to Promote Your Business

You don’t need money — just creativity and consistency.

  • Word-of-Mouth: Ask happy customers to tell 2 friends
  • WhatsApp: Share photos, offers, and delivery updates
  • Social Media: Post on Facebook or Instagram — even once a week
  • Signage: A clean sign with your name, product, and phone number
  • Google Maps: Get listed so people can find you easily
  • Free Samples: Let people try before they buy
“The best marketing doesn’t feel like marketing. It feels like helping.”

✅ 6. Test, Learn & Improve

Marketing is not one-size-fits-all. Try things and see what works.

  • Try two different prices — which sells more?
  • Post on WhatsApp Monday vs. Friday — when do people respond?
  • Ask customers: “How did you hear about us?”

Remember: Small changes can lead to big results.
